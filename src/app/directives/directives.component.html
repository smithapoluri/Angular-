Angular Directives

>

Angular directives are markers on a DOM elements that tell angular's HTML compiler to attach specific behaviour to a DOM element or to transform dom element and its children.

Much like the components, we can create our own directives as well. There are three kinds of directives in Angular: Components—directives with a template. Structural directives—change the DOM layout by adding and removing DOM elements.(NgFor, Nglf) Attribute directives—change the appearance or behavior of an element, component, or another directive NgStyle > ng generate directive highlight Directives must be declared in Angular Modules in the same manner as components.
